# Preneed Sales Team Framework
## Roles, Lead Buckets & Commission Structure

---

## 1. Role Definitions

### Closer
- **Profile:** Lower base hourly wage, higher deal flow capacity
- **Strength:** Best sellers on the team — handle high-value consultations and convert appointments into funded contracts
- **Compensation Model:** Lower hourly + higher commission split (70% of shared deals)
- **Primary Activity:** Closing appointments set by setters, aftercare specialists, and trade show leads

### Setter
- **Profile:** Higher base hourly wage, focused on appointment generation
- **Strength:** Prospecting, phone outreach, file reviews, qualifying leads
- **Compensation Model:** Higher hourly + kickback from deals their leads produce (30% of shared deals)
- **Primary Activity:** Working archived leads, confirming trade show interest, scheduling closer appointments

### Aftercare Specialist
- **Profile:** Higher base hourly wage, relationship-focused
- **Strength:** Empathy-driven follow-up with families who recently experienced a loss
- **Compensation Model:** Higher hourly + commission split on aftercare-sourced preplanning sales
- **Primary Activity:** Post-funeral family contact, nurturing preplanning conversations from at-need services

---

## 2. Three Buckets of Lead Generation

### Bucket 1: Archived Leads (File Reviews)
- **Source:** Existing contact files — families who have interacted with the funeral home but were never worked for preneed
- **Volume:** Large backlog of unworked contacts stored on-site
- **Process:**
  1. Setters pull archived files and conduct outreach (phone, mail, etc.)
  2. Setters qualify interest and set appointments
  3. Closers meet with the family and close the preneed contract
- **Commission Split:** 70% Closer / 30% Setter

### Bucket 2: Aftercare (Post-Funeral Preplanning)
- **Source:** Families who just organized a funeral at the funeral home
- **Volume:** Driven by at-need funeral volume — every funeral is a potential aftercare lead
- **Process:**
  1. Funeral directors perform the at-need service
  2. After the service, the family contact is handed off to the Aftercare Specialist
  3. Aftercare Specialist builds the relationship and introduces preplanning
  4. If the family is interested, the Aftercare Specialist either closes the deal themselves or hands off to a Closer for complex cases
- **Commission Split:** Aftercare Specialist retains their commission share; Funeral Directors receive a referral kickback for sourcing the lead

### Bucket 3: Trade Shows & Outbound Marketing
- **Source:** Community events, senior expos, health fairs, direct mail campaigns, digital marketing
- **Volume:** Seasonal and campaign-driven — spikes around events
- **Process:**
  1. Marketing team or leadership books trade show booths and runs campaigns
  2. Interested contacts are captured and routed to the sales team
  3. Setters follow up, qualify, and set appointments
  4. Closers close the appointments
- **Commission Split:** 70% Closer / 30% Setter

## 3. Commission Split Summary

| Lead Source | Closer | Setter | Aftercare | FD Referral |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Archived Leads (Bucket 1) | 70% | 30% | -- | -- |
| Aftercare Leads (Bucket 2) | -- | -- | Specialist Share | FD Kickback |
| Trade Show / Outbound (Bucket 3) | 70% | 30% | -- | -- |

**Note:** The 70/30 split applies to the agent commission earned on deals where a setter sources the lead and a closer converts it. Bonuses (monthly, annual) are earned individually based on each person's attributed volume.

## 5. Key Design Principles

1. **Setters don't sell** — they generate and qualify. Their compensation reflects consistent hourly pay with upside from lead kickbacks.
2. **Closers are optimized for conversion** — lower base, higher variable comp rewards deal flow and closing ability.
3. **Aftercare is relationship-first** — the specialist earns on the trust they build with grieving families. No hard sell.
4. **Every lead bucket feeds the same pipeline** — regardless of source, leads flow through a consistent qualify-then-close process.
5. **Funeral Directors are incentivized to refer** — the FD kickback on aftercare leads encourages warm handoffs without burdening directors with sales responsibilities.

---

## 6. Staffing Considerations

- **Closer:Setter ratio** — Depends on appointment volume. Closers should stay busy with qualified appointments, not prospecting.
- **Aftercare headcount** — Tied to funeral volume. Each specialist can handle a defined number of family follow-ups per month.
- **Cross-training** — Aftercare specialists may occasionally close their own deals; setters may handle simple renewals. The framework is flexible.
