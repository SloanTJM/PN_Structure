## 1. Untapped Market — The Leads Already Exist

Here's the part most preneed shops can't offer: **TJM's funeral homes have never had a dedicated sales team.** That means:

- **Years of archived family contacts** sitting in files — families who walked through the door for at-need services and were never contacted about preplanning.
- **Every funeral generates aftercare leads** — families in the grief process who are statistically the most likely to preplan for themselves.

You're not inheriting a burned-out lead list. You're walking into a **completely unworked pipeline** with years of accumulated contacts. Most salespeople spend half their career fighting over recycled leads. Here, the field is wide open.

## 2. Setters Fill Your Calendar — You Just Close

TJM's model separates prospecting from closing. A dedicated **setter team** handles all outbound calls, file reviews, and appointment scheduling. Their entire job is to fill your calendar with qualified, confirmed appointments.

**Your day as a closer:**
- Show up to pre-set appointments with families who already expressed interest
- Sit across the table, understand their needs, match them to the right product
- Close the contract and move to the next appointment

**What you don't do:**
- Cold call
- Pull files and prospect
- Chase no-shows and reschedule

**80% of your leads come pre-set.** The setter earns a commission split (20%) on deals they source, so they're incentivized to send you quality appointments — not just fill your calendar with junk. The remaining 20% is your own direct production where you keep 100% of the commission.

## 3. A Product for Every Customer — Nobody Walks Away

Most preneed shops hand you one product and say "go sell it." TJM gives you **six distinct routes** based on customer health and payment preference:

- **Healthy customers**: Single-Pay Whole Life, Multi-Pay Whole Life, or Trust + Interest
- **Health questions**: Single-Pay Annuity or Graded Death Benefit
- **Terminal**: Single-Pay Annuity or Trust + Interest

Healthy, sick, or terminal — pay-in-full or monthly — there's always a product that fits. More products means more closes, fewer walk-aways. You never have to tell a family "sorry, we can't help you."

## 9. Three Lead Buckets Keep the Pipeline Full

Your production isn't dependent on a single lead source. TJM feeds the pipeline from three directions:

### Bucket 1: Archived Leads (The Goldmine)
Years of unworked family contacts from funeral home records. Setters pull files, make calls, qualify interest, and set your appointments. These are families who already know and trust the TJM brand.

### Bucket 2: Aftercare Referrals
Every funeral creates an aftercare opportunity. A dedicated aftercare specialist builds the relationship with grieving families and either closes the preplanning conversation or hands warm leads to you. Funeral directors receive a referral kickback, keeping the handoff pipeline flowing.

### Bucket 3: Trade Shows & Outbound Marketing
Community events, senior expos, health fairs, and direct mail campaigns generate fresh leads. Setters follow up, qualify, and fill your calendar.

Three buckets means your schedule stays full even if one source has a slow month.
